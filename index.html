Usage of English tenses

Verbs come in three tenses: past, present, and future. Read to learn more:

Present Simple

We use the Present Simple tense when an action happens regularly, it is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements.

Present Continuous

We use the Present Continuous for things that are happening at the moment of speaking.

Present Perfect

The present perfect is often used for an action that started at some time in the past and is still continuing now or has a result in present.

Past Simple

We use it with finished actions, states or habits in the past when we have a finished time word (yesterday, last week, at 2 o'clock, in 2003).

Past Continuous

We use it to show that something continued for some time or at a particular time.

Past Perfect

It is used to make it clear that one event happened before another in the past.

  • are travelling
  • travel

3. We ______ (to have) a cool party last night.

  • had
  • have had
  • was having
